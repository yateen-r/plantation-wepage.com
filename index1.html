Plantation

  • Definition
  • Importance
  • Related Schemes

Definition

Plantation refers to the process of planting trees, shrubs, or other vegetation in an area to create a forest or improve the environment. It often involves large-scale reforestation and afforestation efforts to restore degraded lands, improve air quality, and enhance biodiversity.

Importance of Plantation

  • Environmental Benefits: Plantations help in reducing carbon dioxide levels, improving air quality, and mitigating climate change. They also contribute to soil conservation and water regulation.
  • Biodiversity: Planting a diverse range of species can enhance biodiversity, providing habitat for wildlife and preserving various plant species.
  • Economic Benefits: Plantations can provide timber, fruits, nuts, and other products, contributing to the economy and providing employment opportunities.
  • Social Benefits: Green spaces created by plantations can improve the quality of life, offering recreational areas and enhancing the beauty of the landscape.

Schemes That Are Started on Plantation

  • National Afforestation Programme (NAP): A government scheme aimed at promoting afforestation and eco-restoration of degraded forests.
  • Green India Mission: Part of India's National Action Plan on Climate Change, this mission aims to increase forest and tree cover and restore degraded ecosystems.
  • Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA): This scheme includes provisions for tree plantation as part of its employment generation activities.
  • Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (CAMPA): This authority manages funds for compensatory afforestation, ensuring the replacement of forest land diverted for non-forest purposes.
